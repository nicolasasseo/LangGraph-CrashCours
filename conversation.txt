AI: Galois theory is a branch of abstract algebra that provides a profound connection between field theory and group theory. It primarily deals with the solutions of polynomial equations and the symmetries of their roots.

### Key Concepts:

1. **Field Extensions**: A field extension is a larger field that contains a smaller field. For example, the complex numbers are an extension of the real numbers. Galois theory studies the relationships between different field extensions.

2. **Galois Groups**: The Galois group of a field extension is a group consisting of all field automorphisms (bijective functions that preserve the field operations) of the extension that fix the base field. The structure of this group reveals critical information about the polynomial equations associated with the extension.

3. **Polynomial Roots**: One of the central questions in Galois theory is determining whether a polynomial can be solved by radicals (i.e., expressed using a finite number of operations involving only addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, and taking roots). Galois theory establishes that the solvability of polynomials is closely tied to the structure of their Galois groups.

4. **The Fundamental Theorem of Galois Theory**: This theorem states that there is a correspondence between subfields of a field extension and subgroups of the Galois group. This correspondence helps in understanding how the roots of polynomials can be separated or combined.

5. **Abelian Extensions**: Galois theory classifies certain types of field extensions. An extension is called abelian if its Galois group is an abelian group (i.e., the group operation is commutative). The Abel-Ruffini theorem states that there is no general solution in radicals for polynomials of degree five or higher when their Galois group is non-abelian.

### Historical Context:

Galois theory is named after Évariste Galois, a French mathematician who made significant contributions to the field in the early 19th century. His work laid the foundation for much of modern algebra and helped establish the link between different areas of mathematics.

### Applications:

- **Solving Polynomial Equations**: By understanding the Galois group, mathematicians can determine whether a polynomial can be solved by radicals.
- **Number Theory**: Galois theory plays an important role in various areas of number theory, such as in the study of cyclotomic fields and the proof of Fermat's Last Theorem.
- **Cryptography**: Concepts from Galois theory are also used in cryptography, particularly in the construction of certain types of error-correcting codes.
